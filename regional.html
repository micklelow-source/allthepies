Boston Cream Pie

Sweet · Massachusetts, 1856

Technically a cake, but "pie" by tradition — two sponge layers around vanilla custard, glazed with chocolate. Created at Boston's Parker House hotel in 1856 and the official dessert of Massachusetts.

Serves 10Prep 40 minBake 25 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 cups vanilla pastry cream
  • 4 oz dark chocolate
  • ½ cup cream, for glaze

Method

  1. Beat eggs and sugar until pale; fold in flour, baking powder, and milk. Divide between two 9-inch pans.
  2. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20–25 minutes; cool completely.
  3. Spread pastry cream over one layer and top with the second.
  4. Warm chocolate with cream into a smooth glaze; pour over the top and let it drip down the sides.
Parker House, Boston, 1856

Kentucky Chocolate-Walnut Pie

Sweet · Kentucky, 1950s

A bourbon-spiked chocolate and walnut pie in the style made famous for the Kentucky Derby. The original trademarked name belongs to one Louisville family's bakery, so home versions go by descriptions like this one.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup flour
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tbsp bourbon
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and flour, then beat in eggs and melted butter.
  2. Stir in chocolate chips, walnuts, and bourbon.
  3. Pour into the crust.
  4. Bake at 325°F (165°C) for 40–45 minutes until set at the edges but still slightly gooey in the center. Cool before slicing.

Mississippi Mud Pie

Sweet · Mississippi

Chocolate on chocolate on chocolate — a cookie-crumb crust, dense chocolate filling, and whipped cream, named for the dark, silty banks of the Mississippi River.

Serves 10Prep 30 minChill 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 8 oz dark chocolate
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ cup butter (filling)
  • 2 cups whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Mix cookie crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and chill.
  2. Melt chocolate with the filling butter; beat in sugar and eggs.
  3. Pour into the crust and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25 minutes until just set; cool, then chill 4 hours.
  4. Top with whipped cream and chocolate shavings before serving.

Sour Cream Raisin Pie

Sweet · Iowa & the Midwest

A tangy sour cream custard packed with plump raisins under fluffy meringue — a fixture of church potlucks and still a category at the Iowa State Fair baking contests.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 15 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups sour cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 3 egg yolks (reserve whites)
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 3 egg whites + 6 tbsp sugar (meringue)
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Cook sour cream, sugar, flour, and yolks over medium heat, stirring, until thick; stir in raisins and cinnamon.
  2. Pour into the baked crust.
  3. Beat egg whites with sugar to stiff, glossy peaks; spread over the filling, sealing to the crust.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 12–15 minutes until the meringue is golden. Chill before slicing.

Natchitoches Meat Pie

Savory · Louisiana

The official state meat pie of Louisiana: a crimped, empanada-like hand pie of spiced beef and pork, blending Spanish, French, Cajun, and Southern influences. Celebrated with its own annual festival in Natchitoches.

Makes 10Prep 35 minFry/Bake 25 min

Ingredients

  • ½ lb ground beef
  • ½ lb ground pork
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 3 scallions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp flour (to bind)
  • 1 tsp cayenne, 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Brown beef, pork, onion, and scallions with the cayenne and pepper; stir in flour to bind, then cool.
  2. Cut dough into circles; fill each, fold into a half-moon, and crimp firmly with a fork.
  3. Brush with beaten egg.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes until golden, or deep-fry until crisp.

Marionberry Pie

Sweet · Oregon

Built on the marionberry — a blackberry hybrid developed at Oregon State University and now the state's signature berry. Deep, wine-dark, and more complex than a plain blackberry.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 55 min

Ingredients

  • 6 cups marionberries (or blackberries)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss berries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with a lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 minutes more, until thickly bubbling.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Huckleberry Pie

Sweet · Montana & the Mountain West

Made from wild huckleberries foraged in the mountains of Big Sky Country — tarter and more intense than a blueberry, and famously resistant to cultivation, so nearly all are still hand-picked.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 5 cups huckleberries (or wild blueberries)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Pinch cinnamon
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss huckleberries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and cinnamon.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice or a vented crust.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until the juices bubble thickly.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours so the filling sets.

Concord Grape Pie

Sweet · New York (Finger Lakes)

A deep-purple autumn pie from New York's Finger Lakes grape country — intensely grapey, made by slipping the skins off each Concord grape and cooking the pulp before recombining.

Serves 8Prep 45 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 5 cups Concord grapes
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup flour
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Slip the skins off the grapes, reserving them. Simmer the pulp 5 minutes, then sieve out the seeds.
  2. Combine the seedless pulp with the reserved skins, sugar, flour, and lemon juice.
  3. Fill a crust-lined dish, dot with butter, and top with lattice.
  4. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes more. Cool before slicing.

Bakewell Tart

Sweet · Derbyshire, England

Shortcrust pastry, a layer of raspberry jam, and almond frangipane topped with flaked almonds. It evolved in the early 1900s from the older Bakewell pudding — itself said to be a 19th-century kitchen accident at a Bakewell inn.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1 single shortcrust pastry shell
  • ⅓ cup raspberry jam
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • ½ tsp almond extract
  • ¼ cup flaked almonds

Method

  1. Spread the jam over the base of the pastry shell.
  2. Cream butter and sugar, beat in eggs, then fold in ground almonds and almond extract.
  3. Spread the frangipane over the jam and scatter with flaked almonds.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30–35 minutes until golden and set. Serve warm or cool.

Historical note: The first printed Bakewell pudding recipe appeared in Eliza Acton's 1845 Modern Cookery for Private Families; the shortcrust-and-frangipane tart is the 20th-century descendant.

Pastel de Nata

Sweet · Lisbon, Portugal

Portugal's iconic custard tart: crisp, blistered puff pastry cradling a caramelized egg custard, dusted with cinnamon. First made by monks in Belém, Lisbon, before the 18th century — from the yolks left over after egg whites were used to starch laundry and clarify wine.

Makes 12Prep 30 minBake 15 min

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • 1 cup milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 1 strip lemon peel
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Method

  1. Roll the puff pastry into a log, slice into 12, and press each into a muffin cup, pushing dough up the sides.
  2. Whisk flour into a little of the milk; heat the rest with sugar, lemon peel, and cinnamon, then combine and cook until thick.
  3. Off heat, whisk in the yolks; strain, then fill each pastry cup.
  4. Bake at 475°F (245°C) — as hot as your oven allows — for 12–15 minutes until the tops are scorched and blistered.

Historical note: Only tarts from the original Belém bakery may be called pastéis de Belém; its exact recipe is a closely guarded secret. Everywhere else, they're pastéis de nata.

Karelian Pie (Karjalanpiirakka)

Savory · Karelia, Finland

Thin rye pastry pinched into a little "canoe" around a creamy rice-porridge filling, brushed with butter and served with munavoi (egg butter). So culturally specific it holds EU Traditional Speciality Guaranteed status — only pies made the traditional way may use the name.

Makes 12Prep 50 minBake 15 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups rye flour
  • ½ cup water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ⅔ cup short-grain rice
  • 2½ cups milk
  • 4 tbsp butter, melted (to brush)
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs + 3 tbsp butter (egg butter)

Method

  1. Simmer rice in the milk 40 minutes into a thick porridge; cool.
  2. Mix rye flour, water, and salt into a stiff dough; divide into 12 and roll each paper-thin into an oval.
  3. Spread porridge down the center of each; fold and crimp the long edges up, leaving the filling exposed.
  4. Bake at 475°F (245°C) for 12–15 minutes, then brush hot with melted butter. Serve topped with mashed egg-and-butter.

Historical note: First recorded in the 1680s in the Karelia region spanning Finland and Russia; the name evokes the pie's canoe-like shape.

Russian Cabbage Pirog

Savory · Russia

A large enriched-yeast-dough pie filled with buttery braised cabbage and egg — the classic savory pirog, so central to Russian home cooking that baking a good one was once a mark of a capable housekeeper.

Serves 8Prep 40 min + riseBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 3½ cups flour
  • 1 packet yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 3 tbsp butter (dough)
  • 1 small cabbage, shredded
  • 4 tbsp butter (filling)
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten (glaze)

Method

  1. Make a soft yeast dough with flour, yeast, milk, and butter; let rise until doubled.
  2. Braise the cabbage in butter until soft and golden; cool and fold in the chopped egg.
  3. Roll two-thirds of the dough into a base, add the filling, and cover with the remaining dough; seal and glaze.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes until deep gold.

Galaktoboureko

Sweet · Greece

A Greek custard pie: semolina custard wrapped in crisp, butter-brushed phyllo, then soaked in citrus-scented syrup while still hot. The name roughly means "milk pastry."

Serves 12Prep 40 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk
  • ¾ cup fine semolina
  • ¾ cup sugar (custard)
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • ¾ cup butter, melted
  • 1½ cups sugar + 1 cup water + lemon (syrup)

Method

  1. Heat milk, whisk in semolina and sugar until thick; off heat, beat in eggs. Cool slightly.
  2. Layer half the phyllo in a buttered dish, brushing each sheet; add custard, then top with remaining buttered phyllo.
  3. Score into pieces and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40–45 minutes until golden.
  4. Boil the syrup ingredients and pour over the hot pie; let it soak and cool before serving.

Yorkshire Curd Tart

Sweet · Yorkshire, England

A shortcrust tart filled with curd cheese, currants, sugar, and nutmeg. Historically made at Whitsuntide fairs with beestings — the rich first milk of a newly calved cow — with curd cheese as the modern stand-in.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1 single shortcrust pastry shell
  • 1½ cups curd cheese (or ricotta)
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup currants
  • ½ tsp nutmeg

Method

  1. Beat curd cheese with sugar, melted butter, and eggs until smooth.
  2. Stir in the currants and nutmeg.
  3. Pour into the pastry shell.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30–35 minutes until set and lightly golden. Serve warm or cold.

Gypsy Tart

Sweet · Kent, England

Just two ingredients in the filling — evaporated milk and dark muscovado sugar, whipped until light and baked into a deep caramel. A Kentish classic and a fixture of British school dinners from the 1960s to the 1980s.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 12 min

Ingredients

  • 1 single shortcrust pastry shell, blind-baked
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk, chilled
  • 1⅓ cups dark muscovado sugar

Method

  1. Whisk the chilled evaporated milk and muscovado sugar together on high speed for a full 10–12 minutes, until thick, fluffy, and light coffee-colored.
  2. Pour into the blind-baked pastry shell.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for just 10–12 minutes — it should still look slightly wobbly and sticky.
  4. Cool completely; the filling sets to a soft fudge as it stands.

Flamiche aux Poireaux

Savory · Picardy, France

A leek pie from the Picardy region of northern France (and neighboring Belgium): sweet, slow-cooked leeks bound in a light cream-and-egg custard, in a savory pastry shell — a rustic cousin of quiche.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 4 large leeks, sliced
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup crème fraîche or heavy cream
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Sweat the leeks gently in butter 15–20 minutes until meltingly soft, without browning; cool.
  2. Whisk eggs, cream, nutmeg, salt, and pepper, then stir in the leeks.
  3. Pour into the blind-baked crust.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 minutes until set and golden. Serve warm.

New Hampshire Maple Pie

Sweet · New Hampshire

A single-crust custard built entirely on real maple syrup — New England's oldest sweetener, tapped from sugar maples every March using a method colonists learned from Indigenous communities.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups pure maple syrup
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 4 tbsp butter, melted
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk maple syrup and flour, then beat in eggs and melted butter.
  2. Scatter walnuts in the crust, if using, and pour in the filling.
  3. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes until puffed and set with a slight wobble.
  4. Cool at least an hour before slicing — the filling firms as it cools.

Jersey Tomato Pie

Savory-sweet · New Jersey

A summer showcase for the Garden State's famously sweet, sun-ripened tomatoes: sliced thick over a mustard-and-mayo base, baked until just set, with a crumb topping that keeps it somewhere between a pie and a tart.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe Jersey tomatoes, thickly sliced
  • 1 tsp salt (to drain tomatoes)
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Salt the tomato slices and drain on paper towels for 15 minutes.
  2. Spread mayonnaise and mustard over the blind-baked crust.
  3. Layer in the tomatoes, scatter with cheddar and basil.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes until the cheese is bubbling and golden. Cool slightly before slicing.

Rhode Island Ricotta Pie

Sweet · Rhode Island

A cheesecake-like Easter tradition from Providence's large Italian-American community — sweet ricotta, citrus zest, and a hint of cinnamon in a shortcrust shell.

Serves 10Prep 20 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lb whole-milk ricotta
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 lemon & 1 orange, zested
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Beat ricotta with sugar until smooth; beat in eggs one at a time, then flour, zests, and cinnamon.
  2. Pour into the crust.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until set with a slight jiggle.
  4. Cool completely, then chill before slicing.

Vermont Apple Pie with Cheddar

Sweet · Vermont

Vermont's genuinely official state pie — a classic double-crust apple pie, served the New England way, with a slice of sharp cheddar melting into each warm piece. As the old rhyme goes, apple pie without cheese is like a kiss without a squeeze.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6–7 Vermont apples, peeled, sliced
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 8 oz sharp Vermont cheddar, sliced, to serve

Method

  1. Toss apples with sugar, flour, and cinnamon.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish, top with a second crust, seal, and vent.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes more.
  4. Serve warm with a slice of sharp cheddar laid right on top of each piece.

Fact check: Vermont has formally designated apple pie its official state pie — one of the few states where "state pie" isn't an informal title.

Arkansas Possum Pie

Sweet · Arkansas

No possum anywhere near it — the name comes from "playing possum," since a thick layer of whipped cream hides the rich chocolate and cream cheese layers underneath. A pecan crust and toasted pecans finish it off.

Serves 10Prep 30 minChill 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 1 single pecan shortcrust shell, baked
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups chocolate pudding
  • 2 cups whipped cream, divided
  • ½ cup toasted pecans, chopped

Method

  1. Beat cream cheese with powdered sugar and fold in 1 cup whipped cream; spread over the baked crust.
  2. Spread the chocolate pudding over the cream cheese layer.
  3. Top with the remaining whipped cream, sealing to the edges.
  4. Chill at least 4 hours; scatter with toasted pecans before serving.

Chesapeake Crab Pie

Savory · Maryland

Maryland's signature blue crab, lightly bound with egg and a touch of Old Bay, baked in a single crust — a dressier, sliceable cousin of the crab cake.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lump crabmeat
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1½ tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded gruyère or cheddar
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Whisk eggs, cream, and Old Bay together.
  2. Scatter crabmeat, scallions, and half the cheese in the blind-baked crust.
  3. Pour the egg mixture over and top with the remaining cheese.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30–35 minutes until puffed and set. Cool 10 minutes before slicing.

North Carolina Sweet Potato Pie

Sweet · North Carolina

North Carolina has led the country in sweet potato production for decades, and its version of this Southern staple leans on brown sugar and warm spice rather than the corn syrup shortcuts some recipes use.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mashed cooked North Carolina sweet potato
  • ⅔ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp cinnamon, ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Beat sweet potato with brown sugar, milk, eggs, butter, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth.
  2. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until set with a slight jiggle at the center.
  4. Cool before slicing; good with whipped cream.

Oklahoma Fried Pies

Sweet · Oklahoma

Hand-held, deep-fried half-moon pies filled with fruit, born in the Arbuckle Mountains where ranchers fried them over open campfires. Still sold at truck stops and roadside stands across the state.

Makes 8Prep 30 minFry 4 min each

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried apples or peaches, chopped
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 double pie crusts, cut into 8 circles
  • 3 cups oil, for frying

Method

  1. Simmer dried fruit in water to cover until soft, drain, then mash with sugar and cinnamon.
  2. Spoon filling onto half of each dough circle, fold over, and crimp the edge tightly with a fork.
  3. Fry in 350°F (175°C) oil, 2 minutes per side, until deep golden.
  4. Drain on paper towels and cool slightly before eating.

Tennessee Chocolate Pecan Pie

Sweet · Tennessee

A Lynchburg specialty: chocolate chips and pecans in a rich corn-syrup filling, given a shot of Tennessee whiskey — a boozier cousin of pecan pie that trades on the state's most famous export.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 tbsp Tennessee whiskey
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 cup pecan halves
  • ¾ cup chocolate chips
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk corn syrup, sugar, eggs, whiskey, and melted butter until smooth.
  2. Scatter pecans and chocolate chips in the crust, then pour the filling over.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until set with a slight jiggle.
  4. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing.

Texas Pecan Pie

Sweet · Texas

Named the official state pie of Texas by the legislature in 2013 — fitting, since the pecan is also the state's official tree and nut, and pecan trees grow commercially on roughly 70,000 acres across the state.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1½ cups Texas pecan halves
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla until smooth.
  2. Arrange pecans in the crust and pour the filling over.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until set with a slight center jiggle.
  4. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing.

Fact check: House Concurrent Resolution 32 named pecan pie the official state pie of Texas in 2013.

Washington, D.C. Cherry Pie

Sweet · Washington, D.C.

The cherry has been D.C.'s official fruit since 2006, honoring the National Cherry Blossom Festival — born from a 1912 gift of 3,000 cherry trees from the mayor of Tokyo.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 5 cups pitted sour cherries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss cherries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with a lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until thickly bubbling.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Battleship Missouri Pie

Sweet · Missouri

A custard-like pie named for the USS Missouri, said to have been served to President Harry Truman — a Missouri native — by his Master Chief Steward Harry Hightower.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup flour
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and flour, then beat in eggs.
  2. Stir in melted butter, buttermilk, and vanilla.
  3. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40–45 minutes until golden and set with a slight wobble.

Nebraska Tin Roof Sundae Pie

Sweet · Nebraska

An ice cream parlor classic turned into a frozen pie: a chocolate cookie crust, vanilla ice cream, hot fudge, and peanuts — the "tin roof sundae" reimagined for a fork.

Serves 10Prep 25 minFreeze 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1½ qt vanilla ice cream, softened
  • ¾ cup hot fudge sauce
  • 1 cup salted peanuts, chopped
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Mix cookie crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and freeze 15 minutes.
  2. Spread half the softened ice cream in the crust, drizzle with half the fudge and half the peanuts.
  3. Repeat with the remaining ice cream, fudge, and peanuts; freeze at least 4 hours.
  4. Top with whipped cream just before serving.

Ohio Buckeye Pie

Sweet · Ohio

Named for the buckeye candy — a peanut butter ball dipped partway in chocolate to resemble the nut of Ohio's state tree. This pie layers the same chocolate-and-peanut-butter combination into a chocolate cookie crust.

Serves 10Prep 25 minChill 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups whipped cream, divided
  • 4 oz chocolate, melted, to finish

Method

  1. Mix cookie crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and chill.
  2. Beat peanut butter, cream cheese, and powdered sugar until smooth; fold in half the whipped cream.
  3. Spread into the crust and top with the remaining whipped cream.
  4. Chill at least 4 hours; drizzle with melted chocolate before serving.

South Dakota Kuchen

Sweet · South Dakota

"Kuchen" is German for cake, but South Dakota's version — a sweet yeasted crust with a custard filling, often topped with fruit — is close enough to a pie to earn a spot here. Brought by Volga German settlers in the 1880s and named the official state dessert in 2000.

Serves 8Prep 30 min + riseBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 packet yeast
  • ⅓ cup warm milk
  • 4 tbsp butter, softened
  • 2 peaches or apples, sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 eggs, ¼ cup sugar (custard)

Method

  1. Make a soft yeast dough with flour, yeast, milk, and butter; let rise 45 minutes, then press into a pie dish.
  2. Arrange the fruit over the dough.
  3. Whisk cream, eggs, and sugar; pour over the fruit.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes until the custard is set and the edges are golden.

Wisconsin Cranberry Pie

Sweet · Wisconsin

Cranberry has been Wisconsin's official state fruit since 2003, and for good reason — the state leads the nation in cranberry production. Orange zest balances the berries' natural tartness.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh cranberries
  • 1¼ cups sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 orange, zested and juiced
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss cranberries with sugar, cornstarch, orange zest, and juice; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes more.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Alaska Wild Berry Pie

Sweet · Alaska

A mix of blueberries, salmonberries, and crowberries foraged during Alaska's short, intense arctic summer — locals famously guard their picking spots. Any wild-berry mix will do at home.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 5 cups mixed wild berries (blueberry, blackberry, raspberry)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss berries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until thickly bubbling.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours so the filling sets.

Savory alternative: Alaska also has perok, a Russian-influenced salmon pie layered with cabbage, rice, and onion — worth trying if you'd rather go savory.

Arizona Prickly Pear Pie

Sweet · Arizona

Made from the vivid magenta fruit of the desert prickly pear cactus — spines carefully removed before the fruit is peeled and pureed. The flavor sits somewhere between watermelon and bubblegum.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups prickly pear puree (from ~8 fruits, seeded)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Simmer prickly pear puree with sugar, cornstarch, and lime juice until thickened.
  2. Pour into a crust-lined dish and top with a lattice or vented crust.
  3. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes until set.
  4. Cool fully before slicing — the color deepens as it cools.

California Boysenberry Pie

Sweet · California

The boysenberry — a cross of loganberry, raspberry, and blackberry — was developed by farmer Rudolph Boysen in Southern California and popularized by Walter Knott, whose berry stand grew into Knott's Berry Farm.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6 cups boysenberries (or blackberries)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss berries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with a lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 30 minutes more.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Hawaii Haupia Pie

Sweet · Hawaii

A chocolate pudding pie layered with haupia, a traditional Hawaiian coconut milk pudding, and topped with whipped cream — an island twist on the American cream pie.

Serves 8Prep 30 minChill 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar (haupia)
  • 6 tbsp cornstarch (haupia)
  • 2 cups chocolate pudding
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Whisk coconut milk, sugar, and cornstarch in a saucepan; cook over medium heat, stirring, until very thick.
  2. Spread the chocolate pudding in the baked crust, then pour the hot haupia over it and smooth flat.
  3. Chill at least 4 hours until the haupia layer is fully set.
  4. Top with whipped cream before serving.

Nevada Basque Cake Pie

Sweet · Nevada

A tart-like pastry with pastry cream filling, brought by the Basque sheepherders who settled in Nevada from the mid-1800s onward and still celebrated at the state's annual Basque festivals.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups flour
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 eggs (dough)
  • 2 cups pastry cream or thick jam
  • 1 egg, beaten (glaze)
  • Powdered sugar, to finish

Method

  1. Cream butter and sugar, beat in eggs, then work in flour to a soft dough; chill 30 minutes.
  2. Press two-thirds of the dough into a pie dish; fill with pastry cream or jam.
  3. Roll the remaining dough as a lid, seal the edges, and brush with beaten egg.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 35–40 minutes until golden. Dust with powdered sugar to serve.

New Mexico Green Chile Apple Pie

Sweet · New Mexico

Classic apple pie with a jolt of roasted Hatch green chile folded in — sweet, tart, and gently smoky-hot all at once. A distinctly New Mexican twist on the country's most familiar pie.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6 apples, peeled, sliced
  • ⅓ cup roasted Hatch green chile, diced
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss apples with green chile, sugar, flour, cinnamon, and lemon juice.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish, top with a second crust, seal, and vent.
  3. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 30 minutes more.
  4. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing.
