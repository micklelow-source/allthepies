Savory pies through history

8 recipes

Before pie meant dessert, it meant dinner. For most of its 2,000-year history the pie was a savory object — a way to cook, carry, and preserve meat. These eight trace that line from Rome to the Victorian hunt table. (More medieval and colonial savory pies live on the Historical page.)

Roman Patina de Piscibus

Savory · Rome, 1st century AD

A fish-and-shellfish pie in the spirit of Apicius, the 1st-century Roman recipe collection — white fish, mussels, and oysters bound with egg and the fermented fish sauce garum, baked in a plain flour-and-oil crust. The Romans are credited with the first true top-and-bottom pies.

Serves 6Prep 30 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb white fish, cubed
  • 1 cup cooked mussels & oysters
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (garum) or anchovy paste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp lovage or celery leaf, ½ tsp pepper
  • 2 cups flour + ⅓ cup olive oil (crust)

Method

  1. Make a rough dough from flour, olive oil, and a little water; line a dish.
  2. Poach the fish briefly, then layer with the shellfish in the crust.
  3. Beat eggs with fish sauce, lovage, and pepper; pour over the seafood.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 35–40 minutes until set. Serve warm — the Romans would finish it with more pepper.

Historical note: Apicius recorded seafood-in-pastry dishes but rarely gave quantities — cooks were assumed to be professionals who already knew the proportions.

recreated from Apicius, c. 1st century AD

Medieval Eel & Lamprey Pie

Savory · England, 15th century

Fish pies filled the many meatless "fasting" days of the medieval calendar, and eel and lamprey were favorites — spiced with ginger and saffron in a tall coffyn crust. Lamprey pie was such a delicacy that the city of Gloucester sent one to the monarch at Christmas for centuries.

Serves 6Prep 35 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ lb eel (or firm white fish), in pieces
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • Pinch saffron
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp currants
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 1 tbsp verjuice or white wine vinegar

Method

  1. Season the fish with ginger, saffron, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Line a deep dish with crust; add the fish, dot with butter, and scatter currants and verjuice.
  3. Seal with a top crust and cut a vent.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 40–45 minutes. Traditionally eaten cold.

Historical note: Gloucester's tradition of sending a lamprey pie to the Crown dates to at least the 12th century and continues on rare royal occasions to this day.

recreated from 15th-century English fasting-day pies

Tudor Christmas Pie

Savory · England, 16th century

A rich game pie of mixed birds — partridge, chicken, and goose, joined after 1523 by the newly imported turkey — spiced and baked in a sturdy raised crust. The ancestor of the nested "yard-of-birds" showpieces later served at grand houses.

Serves 10Prep 50 minBake 90 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb turkey, boned and cubed
  • ½ lb chicken, boned
  • ½ lb goose or duck, boned
  • 1 tsp mace, 1 tsp pepper
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • 6 tbsp butter
  • 1 hot water crust (4 cups flour, 1 cup lard, 1¼ cups water)
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Toss all the meats with mace, pepper, cloves, and salt.
  2. Make a hot water crust; mold two-thirds up the sides of a tall, lined tin.
  3. Pack in the meats, dot generously with butter, and seal with a crust lid; glaze and vent.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 1½ hours, covering the top if it browns too fast. Cool before slicing.

Historical note: Turkey reached England from the New World around 1523 and was folded into the existing Christmas game pie almost immediately.

recreated from 16th-century English game pies

Pigeon Pie

Savory · England/France, from 1575

Once one of the most common English pies — young pigeons (squab) spiced with nutmeg and cloves, sealed in butter so the cold pie kept for days. An early recipe appears in A Proper Newe Booke of Cokerye (1575); Samuel Pepys recorded a 1667 dinner of "nothing but pigeon-pyes."

Serves 6Prep 30 minBake 60 min

Ingredients

  • 4 squab or 6 chicken thighs, boned
  • 4 oz bacon or salt pork, diced
  • ½ tsp nutmeg, ½ tsp cloves
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, quartered
  • 1 cup stock
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Season the meat with nutmeg, cloves, pepper, and salt.
  2. Layer with bacon and egg in a crust-lined dish; dot with butter and add the stock.
  3. Top with a second crust; seal and vent.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 55–60 minutes until golden. Good hot or cold.

Historical note: Early French-Canadian tourtière was likely made with passenger pigeon (tourte) before the bird was hunted to extinction.

recreated from A Proper Newe Booke of Cokerye, 1575

Melton Mowbray Pork Pie

Savory · Leicestershire, 1700s

The definitive English raised pork pie: hand-formed hot water crust around uncured, coarsely chopped pork (grey when cooked, not pink), with savory jelly poured in through the top after baking. Protected by the EU since 2008 — the real thing must be made near Melton Mowbray.

Serves 6Prep 50 minBake 90 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ lb pork shoulder & belly, hand-chopped
  • 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp pepper
  • ½ tsp ground mace or nutmeg
  • 3 cups flour
  • ¾ cup lard
  • ¾ cup water (crust)
  • 1½ cups pork stock + 1 tsp gelatin (jelly)

Method

  1. Season the chopped pork with salt, pepper, and mace.
  2. Boil lard and water, stir into the flour, and knead into a hot water crust; mold two-thirds around a jar or tin while warm.
  3. Pack in the pork, add a crust lid, seal, glaze, and cut a central hole.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 1½ hours. Once cooled, pour warm jellied stock through the hole; chill until set.

Historical note: Melton's pork pie trade grew from local Stilton cheesemaking — surplus whey fattened the pigs — and from the town's place in prime fox-hunting country.

recreated from the traditional Leicestershire raised pie

Battalia Pie

Savory · England, 17th–18th century

A lavish banquet pie of small delicacies — chicken, pigeon, and rabbit pieces with cockscombs and savory forcemeat balls, bathed in a claret-and-anchovy sauce. Its name is a corruption of the French béatilles ("little tidbits"); Disraeli described one as a "gorgeous sepulchre."

Serves 8Prep 60 minBake 70 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb mixed chicken, rabbit & pigeon, in pieces
  • 12 small forcemeat balls
  • 4 oz mushrooms
  • 2 hard-boiled egg yolks
  • ½ cup claret (red wine)
  • 1 tbsp anchovy paste
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Brown the meats and forcemeat balls lightly; arrange with mushrooms and egg yolks in a crust-lined dish.
  2. Simmer claret, anchovy paste, and butter into a rich sauce; pour over.
  3. Top with a decorated crust; seal and vent.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 60–70 minutes until deep gold.

Historical note: Battalia pie descends from an elaborate medieval Forme of Cury pie with pastry "battlements" — the crenellated crust that gave the dish its warlike-sounding name.

recreated from 17th–18th century English banquet pies

Scotch Pie (Mutton Pie)

Savory · Scotland, 18th–19th century

A small, straight-sided double-crust pie of highly peppered minced mutton in hot water pastry, its top set low to hold gravy, beans, or mash. Grew from the "penny mutton pies" sold to working men; now a football-terrace institution with its own annual world championship.

Makes 6Prep 40 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb minced mutton or lamb
  • ½ tsp ground mace
  • 1 tsp pepper, 1 tsp salt
  • ⅓ cup stock or gravy
  • 3 cups flour
  • ⅔ cup lard or beef dripping
  • ¾ cup water (crust)

Method

  1. Mix the mutton with mace, pepper, salt, and a little stock.
  2. Make a hot water crust; line six small round tins, keeping a third back for lids.
  3. Fill each with meat and top with a pastry lid set slightly below the rim; make a small central hole.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 40–45 minutes. Serve hot, the well on top filled with gravy or beans.

Historical note: Now legally defined — a 1967 UK regulation specifies a Scotch pie's size and mince-and-cereal filling; mutton gave the traditional version its characteristic sweetness.

recreated from the Scottish penny mutton pie

Moroccan Bastilla

Sweet-savory · Morocco

A spectacular Moroccan celebration pie that walks the sweet-savory line: spiced pigeon or chicken and almonds layered in crackling warqa (phyllo-like) pastry, dusted with cinnamon and powdered sugar. A cousin of the pigeon pie carried along Mediterranean trade routes.

Serves 8Prep 60 minBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lb chicken thighs (or pigeon)
  • 2 onions, grated
  • 1 tsp each ginger, cinnamon, saffron
  • 6 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup toasted almonds, ground
  • 1 package phyllo (or warqa)
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • Powdered sugar & cinnamon, to finish

Method

  1. Simmer chicken with onion and spices until tender; shred the meat and set aside.
  2. Reduce the cooking liquid, then scramble the eggs into it until just set.
  3. Layer buttered phyllo in a round dish; add the egg mixture, shredded meat, and ground almonds, then wrap in more buttered phyllo.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes until golden. Dust with powdered sugar and cinnamon before serving.

Historical note: Bastilla (also b'stilla or pastilla) reflects centuries of Andalusian and Arab influence on Moroccan cuisine; it's traditionally served at weddings and feasts.

recreated from the traditional Moroccan celebration pie

British Isles

Where the savory pie tradition never really stopped: pub classics built for a cold night.

Steak & Ale Pie

Savory · England

Chunks of beef braised low and slow in dark ale until the gravy turns glossy and deeply savory, under a puff pastry lid.

Serves 6Prep 30 minBraise 2 hrBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lb beef chuck, cubed
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 12 oz dark ale or stout
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Toss beef in flour; brown well in batches, then set aside.
  2. Soften onions and carrots in the same pot, then return beef.
  3. Add ale, stock, Worcestershire, and bay leaves; cover and braise at 325°F (165°C) for 1¾–2 hours until fork-tender. Cool the filling.
  4. Transfer filling to a pie dish, top with puff pastry, brush with egg, and cut a steam vent.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25–30 minutes until the pastry is puffed and deep gold.

Shepherd's Pie

Savory · Britain/Ireland

Ground lamb (beef makes this "cottage pie") simmered with vegetables and gravy, topped with a thick layer of buttery mashed potato instead of pastry.

Serves 6Prep 25 minBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ lb ground lamb
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef or lamb stock
  • 2 lb potatoes, peeled
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup milk

Method

  1. Brown lamb with onion and carrots; drain excess fat.
  2. Stir in tomato paste and stock; simmer 15 minutes, then fold in peas.
  3. Boil potatoes until tender, then mash with butter and milk until smooth.
  4. Spread meat filling in a baking dish, top with mashed potato, and rough up the surface with a fork.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25–30 minutes until the top is golden in spots.

Cornish Pasty

Savory · Cornwall, England

A hand-held half-moon of beef, potato, swede (rutabaga), and onion, crimped along one edge — designed so a Cornish tin miner could hold it by the crust and eat the rest without washing up.

Makes 6Prep 30 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef skirt or chuck, diced small
  • 2 potatoes, diced small
  • 1 small rutabaga, diced small
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 2 tbsp butter, cubed
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Combine raw beef, potato, rutabaga, and onion; season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Cut crust into six circles. Mound filling on one half of each, dot with butter.
  3. Fold dough over into a half-moon and crimp the curved edge firmly by hand.
  4. Brush with egg and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until deep golden.

Chicken Pot Pie

Savory · United States

Shredded chicken, carrots, peas, and celery bound in a rich, thyme-scented gravy, sealed under a flaky double crust.

Serves 6Prep 25 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • ⅓ cup flour
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • ⅔ cup milk
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • ¾ cup peas
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Melt butter, whisk in flour, and cook 1 minute. Whisk in stock and milk until smooth and thickened.
  2. Add carrots and celery; simmer 8–10 minutes until just tender.
  3. Stir in chicken, peas, and thyme; season to taste and cool slightly.
  4. Fill a crust-lined dish, top with a second crust, seal, and cut steam vents.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 35–40 minutes until deeply golden.

Tourtière

Savory · Québec, Canada

A warmly spiced ground pork (often mixed with beef) pie, traditionally served after midnight Mass on Christmas Eve — le réveillon — in French-Canadian households.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • ½ lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup mashed potato
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Cook pork, beef, onion, and garlic together until browned; drain excess fat.
  2. Stir in mashed potato and spices; simmer 10 minutes until thick enough to hold its shape.
  3. Cool, then fill a crust-lined dish and top with a second crust; seal and vent.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 40–45 minutes until golden.

Tamale Pie

Savory · American Southwest

A Depression-era Tex-Mex casserole-pie: chili-spiced ground beef and corn under a cornbread "crust," inspired by tamale flavors but built for a weeknight.

Serves 6Prep 20 minBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar

Method

  1. Brown beef with onion and chili powder; stir in tomatoes and corn, simmer 10 minutes.
  2. Whisk cornmeal, milk, and egg into a loose batter.
  3. Spread meat mixture in a baking dish, pour cornmeal batter over the top.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes; scatter cheese on top for the last 5 minutes.

Quiche Lorraine

Savory · Lorraine, France

A silky egg-and-cream custard studded with smoky lardons, baked in a single crust — no cheese in the original Lorraine version, though most modern recipes add a little gruyère.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 8 oz bacon or lardons, diced
  • 4 eggs
  • 1½ cups heavy cream
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • ½ cup gruyère, grated (optional)
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Cook bacon until crisp; drain and scatter over the blind-baked crust.
  2. Whisk eggs, cream, nutmeg, and salt together; pour over the bacon.
  3. Top with gruyère if using.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30–35 minutes until just set with a slight wobble in the center.

Spanakopita

Savory · Greece

Spinach and tangy feta wrapped in paper-thin, butter-brushed layers of phyllo, baked until shatteringly crisp.

Serves 8Prep 35 minBake 35 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ lb spinach, wilted and squeezed dry
  • 8 oz feta, crumbled
  • 3 scallions, chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill
  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • ½ cup butter, melted

Method

  1. Mix spinach, feta, scallions, eggs, and dill.
  2. Layer half the phyllo sheets in a baking dish, brushing each with melted butter.
  3. Spread the spinach filling evenly, then layer the remaining phyllo on top, buttering each sheet.
  4. Score the top into squares and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 minutes until golden and crisp.

Torta Pasqualina

Savory · Liguria, Italy

An Easter tradition from Genoa: layers of thin pastry around a spinach-and-ricotta filling with whole eggs nestled inside, sliced to reveal a golden yolk in every piece.

Serves 8Prep 40 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lb spinach or chard, wilted and chopped
  • 15 oz ricotta
  • ½ cup parmesan, grated
  • 2 eggs, for the filling
  • 6 whole eggs, for nestling
  • 2 double pie crusts or 6 thin pastry sheets
  • 3 tbsp olive oil

Method

  1. Mix spinach, ricotta, parmesan, and 2 eggs into a thick filling.
  2. Layer half the pastry sheets into a dish, brushing each with olive oil.
  3. Spread half the filling, make 6 shallow wells, and crack a whole egg into each; cover with remaining filling.
  4. Layer the rest of the pastry on top, sealing the edges well.
  5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 40–45 minutes until deep golden; cool slightly so the eggs finish setting before slicing.

Argentine Beef Empanadas

Savory · Argentina

Hand pies filled with chopped beef, onion, olives, and a hit of cumin and paprika, crimped into a rope edge and baked or fried until crackling.

Makes 12Prep 30 minBake 25 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground or finely chopped beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ cup green olives, chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 12 empanada dough rounds
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Cook beef and onion with cumin and paprika until browned; cool completely.
  2. Stir in olives and chopped egg.
  3. Fill each dough round, fold in half, and crimp the edge into a tight rope pattern.
  4. Brush with beaten egg and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes until golden.

Jamaican Beef Patties

Savory · Jamaica

Spiced ground beef in a strikingly yellow, turmeric-tinted crust, sold from bakery windows across Jamaica and its diaspora.

Makes 10Prep 30 minBake 30 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 1 scotch bonnet or jalapeño, minced
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • 2½ cups flour
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 1 cup cold butter, cubed

Method

  1. Cook beef, onion, chile, curry powder, and thyme until browned; stir in breadcrumbs to bind, then cool.
  2. Combine flour and turmeric; cut in butter and enough cold water to form a firm dough.
  3. Roll dough, cut into circles, fill half of each, fold over, and crimp with a fork.
  4. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25–30 minutes until firm and golden.

Pastel de Choclo

Savory · Chile

A crustless "pie" in the loosest sense: a sweet, thick corn purée baked over a savory beef, olive, and hard-boiled-egg filling until the top turns a deep, caramelized gold.

Serves 6Prep 30 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • ½ cup black olives
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 4 cups corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Method

  1. Brown beef with onion; season, then spread in a baking dish with olives and sliced egg on top.
  2. Blend corn with milk, butter, and sugar until thick and coarse-pureed; cook in a pan, stirring, until it thickens further.
  3. Spread the corn mixture over the meat layer.
  4. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 30–35 minutes until the top is caramelized and golden at the edges.
