Fruit Pies

5 recipes

Unsweetened until sugar became affordable in the 1600s — now the whole point.

Classic Apple Pie

Sweet · United States

First recorded in 14th-century England, adopted and sweetened in America — cinnamon-spiced apples under a golden lattice.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6–7 baking apples, peeled, sliced
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp butter, cubed
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish; dot with butter.
  3. Weave a lattice top (or use a full top crust with vents) and crimp the edge.
  4. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) and bake 30 minutes more until bubbling.
  5. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing so the filling sets.

Cherry Pie

Sweet · United States

Tart, deep-red sour cherries balanced with just enough sugar — a Midwestern and Michigan-orchard classic.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 5 cups pitted sour cherries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ¼ tsp almond extract
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss cherries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and almond extract; let sit 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with a lattice or full crust; crimp edges.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until the filling bubbles thickly through the vents.
  4. Cool fully — at least 3 hours — before slicing for clean pieces.

Blueberry Pie

Sweet · New England, United States

A summer pie built on wild Maine blueberries and a splash of lemon to keep the sweetness in check.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6 cups blueberries
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice, plus zest
  • Pinch cinnamon
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss blueberries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice and zest, and cinnamon.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice or a vented crust.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45–50 minutes until thick purple juice bubbles through the vents.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Peach Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

Ripe summer peaches, barely thickened, so the filling stays loose and juicy rather than jammy.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 6 cups sliced ripe peaches
  • ⅔ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss peaches with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and cinnamon; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes more.
  4. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing.

Strawberry-Rhubarb Pie

Sweet · United States/Britain

Tart rhubarb and sweet strawberries in the classic early-summer pairing sometimes called "pie plant pie."

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 3 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 3 cups strawberries, halved
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp orange zest
  • 2 double pie crusts

Method

  1. Toss rhubarb and strawberries with sugar, cornstarch, and orange zest; rest 20 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish and top with lattice.
  3. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 30 minutes more until thickly bubbling.
  4. Cool fully before slicing.

Custard & Nut Pies

6 recipes

Eggs and dairy (or corn syrup) do the work here instead of fruit — the backbone of the Thanksgiving table and the Southern pie safe alike.

Pumpkin Pie

Sweet · United States

Silky spiced pumpkin custard — absent from the actual first Thanksgiving, but essential to every one since the early 1800s.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 55 min

Ingredients

  • 15 oz pumpkin purée
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ginger
  • ¼ tsp cloves
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk pumpkin, sugar, eggs, evaporated milk, and spices until smooth.
  2. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  3. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes, then reduce to 350°F (175°C) for 40 minutes more, until the center barely jiggles.
  4. Cool fully before slicing.

Pecan Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

A deep, glossy corn-syrup filling packed with toasted pecans — popularized in the 1930s after corn syrup makers printed the recipe on the bottle.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1½ cups pecan halves
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla until smooth.
  2. Arrange pecans in the crust and pour the filling over them.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until the center is set with a slight jiggle.
  4. Cool at least 2 hours before slicing.

Sweet Potato Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

A Southern holiday staple with deep roots in African American culinary tradition — spiced mashed sweet potato in place of pumpkin.

Serves 8Prep 20 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mashed cooked sweet potato
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Beat sweet potato with sugar, milk, eggs, butter, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth.
  2. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45–50 minutes until set with a slight jiggle at the center.
  4. Cool before slicing; serve with whipped cream if you like.

Chess Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

Butter, sugar, and egg, barely more complicated than that — a little cornmeal gives the filling its faint, distinctive grit. The name's origin is genuinely disputed; the leading folk story is a Southern cook shrugging "it's jes' pie."

Serves 8Prep 10 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornmeal
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • ¼ cup buttermilk
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and cornmeal, then beat in eggs one at a time.
  2. Stir in melted butter, buttermilk, vinegar, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40–45 minutes until golden brown and just set.

Buttermilk Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

A close cousin of chess pie, built around tangy buttermilk for a custard that's slightly less sweet and a little more complex.

Serves 8Prep 10 minBake 45 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and flour, then beat in eggs.
  2. Stir in melted butter, buttermilk, and vanilla.
  3. Pour into an unbaked crust.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40–45 minutes until golden and set with a slight wobble.

Shoofly Pie

Sweet · Pennsylvania Dutch

A dense molasses pie topped with a crumbly streusel, sweet enough that legend says you have to shoo the flies away — hence the name.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup molasses
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ¾ cup boiling water
  • 1½ cups flour
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup cold butter, cubed
  • 1 single pie crust

Method

  1. Whisk molasses and baking soda into boiling water until foamy; pour into the crust.
  2. Rub flour, brown sugar, and butter together into coarse crumbs; scatter over the molasses layer.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 35–40 minutes until the top is set and crumbly.

Cream Pies

5 recipes

No baking the filling required — just a stovetop custard, a cool crust, and (usually) a cloud of whipped cream or meringue.

Key Lime Pie

Sweet · Florida Keys, United States

Tart Key lime juice and sweetened condensed milk set into a graham cracker crust — a Florida original dating to the 1850s, once sweetened milk became shelf-stable.

Serves 8Prep 15 minBake 15 min

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 2 cans (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • ⅔ cup Key lime juice
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Mix graham crumbs with melted butter; press into a pie dish and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8 minutes.
  2. Whisk condensed milk, egg yolks, and lime juice until smooth; pour into the crust.
  3. Bake 15 minutes until just set at the edges with a slight center wobble.
  4. Chill at least 3 hours, then top with whipped cream before serving.

Banana Cream Pie

Sweet · United States

Layers of sliced banana and vanilla custard in a baked crust, crowned with whipped cream — a diner-counter classic.

Serves 8Prep 25 minChill 3 hr

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 2½ cups milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3 bananas, sliced
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and cornstarch, then whisk in milk and egg yolks.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thick; remove from heat and stir in butter and vanilla.
  3. Layer banana slices in the baked crust, pour custard over, and press plastic wrap onto the surface.
  4. Chill at least 3 hours; top with whipped cream just before serving.

Chocolate Cream Pie

Sweet · United States

A deep, dark chocolate custard under whipped cream — rich enough that a thin slice is genuinely plenty.

Serves 8Prep 25 minChill 3 hr

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • ⅓ cup cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 2½ cups milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 4 oz dark chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Whisk sugar, cornstarch, and cocoa, then whisk in milk and egg yolks.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring, until thick; off heat, stir in chocolate and butter until smooth.
  3. Pour into the baked crust and press plastic wrap onto the surface.
  4. Chill at least 3 hours; top with whipped cream before serving.

Coconut Cream Pie

Sweet · United States

Toasted coconut folded into vanilla custard, with more toasted coconut scattered over the whipped cream on top.

Serves 8Prep 25 minChill 3 hr

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 2½ cups milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1 cup shredded coconut, toasted, divided
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Whisk sugar and cornstarch, then whisk in milk and egg yolks.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring, until thick; stir in ¾ cup toasted coconut and vanilla.
  3. Pour into the baked crust and press plastic wrap onto the surface; chill at least 3 hours.
  4. Top with whipped cream and the remaining toasted coconut before serving.

Lemon Meringue Pie

Sweet · United States

Sharp lemon curd under a tall, torch-browned meringue — a showpiece pie that's really two separate techniques stacked on one crust.

Serves 8Prep 25 minBake 12 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • ⅓ cup cornstarch
  • 1½ cups water
  • 4 egg yolks (reserve whites)
  • ⅓ cup lemon juice, plus zest
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 4 egg whites (reserved)
  • ½ cup sugar, for meringue
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked

Method

  1. Whisk 1 cup sugar and cornstarch with water; cook over medium heat, stirring, until thick.
  2. Temper in egg yolks, then cook 2 minutes more; stir in lemon juice, zest, and butter. Pour into the baked crust.
  3. Beat egg whites to soft peaks, gradually adding ½ cup sugar until glossy stiff peaks form.
  4. Spread meringue over the hot filling, sealing to the crust edge, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10–12 minutes until golden.
  5. Cool at room temperature, then chill before slicing with a wet knife.

Frozen & icebox pies

6 recipes

No oven required for the filling — just a mixer, a freezer, and patience. Most of these trace to a specific mid-century home cook or restaurant, which makes them some of the best-documented pies on the whole site.

French Silk Pie

Sweet · Maryland, 1951

Not French at all — a whipped, mousse-like chocolate filling invented by home baker Betty Cooper of Kensington, Maryland, who entered it in the third annual Pillsbury Bake-Off in 1951. The name is generally thought to describe the filling's silky texture, not its origin.

Serves 8Prep 30 minChill 6 hr

Ingredients

  • 4 oz unsweetened chocolate, melted
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 4 eggs, pasteurized
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 single pie crust, blind-baked
  • 1½ cups whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Melt the chocolate and let cool slightly.
  2. Beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
  3. Beat in melted chocolate and vanilla, then add eggs one at a time, beating 2 full minutes after each addition, until the filling is pale and mousse-like.
  4. Pour into the baked crust and chill at least 6 hours, or overnight.
  5. Top with whipped cream and chocolate curls before serving.

Historical note: This is an "icebox pie" in the true sense — chilled rather than baked or frozen solid, a category of dessert that boomed in the 1950s as home refrigerators became standard kitchen equipment.

Grasshopper Pie

Sweet · America, late 1940s

A pastel-green, no-bake pie built on the flavors of the Grasshopper cocktail — mint and chocolate liqueur. As the story goes, the cocktail's own inventor, a chemist at a liqueur distillery, had a wife who thickened the drink with melted marshmallows and froze it in a chocolate crumb crust.

Serves 8Prep 25 minFreeze 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 24 large marshmallows
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¼ cup crème de menthe
  • ¼ cup white crème de cacao
  • 1½ cups heavy cream, whipped

Method

  1. Mix cookie crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and chill.
  2. Melt marshmallows with milk over low heat, stirring until smooth; cool completely.
  3. Stir in the crème de menthe and crème de cacao.
  4. Fold in the whipped cream, then pour into the crust.
  5. Freeze at least 4 hours until firm.

Historical note: The Grasshopper cocktail itself is usually traced to either a 1940s liqueur-company chemist or an early-1900s New Orleans bartender — accounts differ, but both agree the pie followed the drink, not the other way around.

Frozen Key Lime Pie

Sweet · Florida

A true-frozen cousin of the baked Key Lime Pie on the Sweet page's cream pie shelf — the same tart, condensed-milk filling, but churned into a soft-set, sliceable ice cream instead of baked into a custard.

Serves 8Prep 15 minFreeze 6 hr

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 2 cans (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ⅔ cup Key lime juice
  • 1 tbsp lime zest

Method

  1. Mix graham crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and freeze 15 minutes.
  2. Whisk condensed milk with lime juice and zest until thickened slightly.
  3. Whip the cream to soft peaks and fold into the lime mixture.
  4. Pour into the crust and freeze at least 6 hours, or until firm.
  5. Let stand 10 minutes at room temperature before slicing.

Frozen Mud Pie

Sweet · San Francisco, 1957

Coffee ice cream, fudge, and an Oreo crust — reportedly invented in 1957 by Joanna Droeger at her Brighton Express restaurant in San Francisco, after she read that a newlywed Barbra Streisand kept a freezer of coffee ice cream under her bed. A customer watching her hand-press ice cream into a crust asked what she was making; "just making mud pies," she said, and the name stuck.

Serves 10Prep 25 minFreeze 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 24 chocolate sandwich cookies, crushed
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1½ qt coffee ice cream, softened
  • ¾ cup hot fudge sauce
  • ½ cup toffee bits, chopped
  • 1 cup whipped cream, to finish

Method

  1. Mix cookie crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and freeze 15 minutes.
  2. Spread half the softened coffee ice cream into the crust; freeze 10 minutes.
  3. Spoon fudge sauce over the ice cream and scatter with toffee; freeze 10 minutes.
  4. Add the remaining ice cream on top and freeze at least 4 hours until firm.
  5. Top with whipped cream just before serving.

Peanut Butter Icebox Pie

Sweet · Southern United States

A no-bake Southern favorite: sweetened cream cheese and peanut butter whipped light, piled into a crumb crust, and chilled until sliceable — a close cousin of the region's chess and buttermilk pies, but built for a hot kitchen with the oven staying off.

Serves 8Prep 20 minChill 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tbsp butter, melted
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups whipped cream, divided
  • ¼ cup chopped peanuts, to finish

Method

  1. Mix graham crumbs and melted butter; press into a pie dish and chill.
  2. Beat cream cheese, peanut butter, and powdered sugar until smooth and fluffy.
  3. Fold in half the whipped cream, then spread into the crust.
  4. Top with the remaining whipped cream and scatter with chopped peanuts.
  5. Chill at least 4 hours before slicing.

Baked Alaska Pie

Sweet · New York, 1867

A frozen ice cream pie wrapped in torched meringue — hot outside, cold inside. Chef Charles Ranhofer of Delmonico's in New York is credited with the concept shortly after the 1867 U.S. purchase of Alaska, originally naming the dish "Alaska, Florida" to play on the contrast between the frozen north and warm south.

Serves 8Prep 30 minFreeze 4 hr

Ingredients

  • 1 single pie crust or cookie crust, baked
  • 1½ qt vanilla ice cream, softened
  • 4 egg whites
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar

Method

  1. Pack the softened ice cream into the baked crust and freeze at least 4 hours until very firm.
  2. Beat egg whites with cream of tartar to soft peaks, then gradually add sugar, beating to stiff, glossy peaks.
  3. Working quickly, spread the meringue over the frozen pie, covering it completely and sealing to the crust edge.
  4. Brown the meringue with a kitchen torch, or under a hot broiler for 1–2 minutes, watching closely.
  5. Serve immediately.
