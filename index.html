Before you dig in

Why "coffyn"?

The word pie almost certainly comes from the magpie — a bird known for hoarding odds and ends in its nest, not unlike the miscellaneous fillings (pigeon, beef, dried fruit, whatever was on hand) stuffed into medieval pastry. That pastry was called a coffyn: a thick, deliberately inedible flour-and-water box, built to hold a filling together over an open fire the way a jar or a basket would. It's the reason pie exists at all — a piece of edible engineering that just happened to taste good along the way. The full story is on the Historical Pies page.
