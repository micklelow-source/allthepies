Sweetheart Cherry Hand Pies

Sweet · Valentine's Day

Individual heart-shaped hand pies with a jammy cherry filling — cut with a heart cookie cutter instead of folded into a half-moon.

Makes 8Prep 25 minBake 20 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups pitted cherries, chopped
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp coarse sugar, for topping

Method

  1. Simmer cherries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice until thick; cool completely.
  2. Cut 16 heart shapes from rolled dough. Spoon filling onto 8 hearts, leaving a border.
  3. Top with the remaining hearts, press and crimp the edges to seal.
  4. Brush with egg, sprinkle with coarse sugar, and cut a small vent in each.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 18–20 minutes until golden.

Star-Spangled Berry Pie

Sweet · Fourth of July

Blueberries and strawberries in one filling, finished with a handful of star shapes cut from the top crust instead of a full lattice.

Serves 8Prep 30 minBake 50 min

Ingredients

  • 3 cups blueberries
  • 3 cups strawberries, halved
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Toss berries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Fill a crust-lined dish. Cut star shapes from the second crust with a small cutter and arrange over the top, overlapping slightly.
  3. Brush with egg and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes more.
  4. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing.

Mincemeat Hand Pies

Sweet · Christmas

The modern, fruit-and-spice-only descendant of the medieval meat mince pie — dried fruit, brandy, and warm spice in a hand-pie shape sized for a holiday dessert table.

Makes 10Prep 25 minBake 20 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed dried fruit (raisins, currants, apricots), chopped
  • 1 apple, grated
  • ⅓ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp brandy or apple juice
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp cloves
  • 2 double pie crusts
  • 1 egg, beaten

Method

  1. Combine dried fruit, apple, brown sugar, brandy or apple juice, and spices; let sit 20 minutes to soften.
  2. Cut dough into 20 circles. Spoon filling onto 10, top with the remaining circles, and crimp with a fork.
  3. Brush with egg and cut a small vent in each.
  4. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 18–20 minutes until golden.

Historical note: Original mince pies contained meat and were shaped oblong, to represent a manger — a piece of folklore modern food historians treat with some skepticism.
